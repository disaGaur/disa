The history of Rajma Chawal

Rājmā[2] [raːdʒmaː] (Hindi: राजमा, Urdu: راجما), also known as rajmah, rāzmā, or lal lobia, is a vegetarian dish, originating from the Indian subcontinent, consisting of red kidney beans in a thick gravy with many Indian whole spices, and is usually served with rice. It is a part of regular diet in India, Nepal, Bangladesh and Punjab province of Pakistan.[1] The dish developed after the red kidney bean was brought to the Indian subcontinent from Mexico.[3] Rajma chawal is kidney beans served with boiled rice.

First let's see the ingredients

  • 1 cup red kidney beans
  • 2 large onion
  • 1 teaspoon ginger
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon powdered black pepper
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 green cardamom
  • 2 clove
  • 2 tablespoon mustard oil
  • 2 large tomato
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 tablespoon garlic
  • 2 chopped green chilli
  • 1 tablespoon cumin powder
  • salt as required
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala powder
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon ghee

Now here comes the recipeeeeeeee!!!

Just follow these steps
  1. Soak rajma overnight and pressure cook till they are soft
    1. Wash the rajma and allow it to soak in water overnight.
    2. Wash it again and pressure cook with 2 cups of water, 1/2 tsp salt and turmeric.
    3. Once the rajma is soft and squishy, remove it from the heat and keep it aside.

  2. Chop onion-tomato and make ginger-garlic paste
    1. Finely chop onions and keep aside, grate the tomatoes and keep them aside.
    2. Make a paste of ginger, garlic and green chillies.

  3. Prepare the rajma
    1. In a deep bottomed pan, add mustard oil, when it gets heated add green cardamom, black cardamom, cinnamon, clove, peppercorn.
    2. Once they become fragrant, add onions and cook till they are golden brown.
    3. Now add the tomatoes and cook on high for 4-5 minutes.
    4. Add ginger- garlic-chilli paste, cumin powder, coriander powder and black pepper powder.
    5. Cook till the spices become fragrant and oil starts leaving the sides of the pan.
    6. Now add the rajma and one cup of water. Mix well and cook covered for 10 minutes.

  4. Steam cook rice and serve with Rajma
